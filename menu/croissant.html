Croissant

Flaky, buttery, and golden brown — the classic French croissant is a breakfast favorite for a reason. With its delicate layers and soft interior, this homemade version brings the taste of a Parisian bakery right to your kitchen. Whether enjoyed plain, filled with chocolate, or paired with your morning coffee, this single-serving recipe is perfect for when you want a fresh croissant without the full batch.

Save Rate Share
Prep Time Cook Time Total Time
20 Mins 4 hrs 40 mins 5 hrs
Serving Yield
1 2-3 mini Croissant

Ingredients

  • 100 g bread flour (about ¾ cup)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp instant yeast
  • 60 ml cold water (about 4 tbsp)
  • 1 tbsp milk (optional, for softer texture)
  • 40 g unsalted butter (cold, shaped into a thin square)
  • 1 egg yolk + 1 tsp milk (mixed together)

Direction

  • Step 1

    Make the Dough Mix flour, sugar, salt, yeast, and water (plus milk if using). Knead until smooth. Chill 30 minutes.

  • Step 2

    Prepare the Butter Flatten cold butter into a small square. Chill.

  • Step 3

    Fold the Butter (Lamination) Roll dough into a rectangle. Place butter in the center, fold dough over it. Roll out, fold into thirds. Chill 20 minutes. Repeat 2–3 times.

  • Step 4

    Shape the Croissants Roll out the dough thin (3–4 mm). Cut into triangles and roll into croissant shape. Let rise for 1–1.5 hours.

  • Step 5

    Bake Brush with egg wash. Bake at 200°C (392°F) for 15–20 minutes until golden.
